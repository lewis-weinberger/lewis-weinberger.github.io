About

This is the personal website of Lewis Weinberger. I am currently an astrophysics PhD student at the University of Cambridge's [Institute of Astronomy]. My research so far has focused on the inter-galactic medium (IGM) — the stuff in-between galaxies — in particular how this medium evolved alongside galaxies in the early Universe.


A lot of my research makes use of High Performance Computing (HPC), running calculations on [supercomputers]. Outside of my PhD work, I'm a computerphile — I enjoy learning about and using programming languages and paradigms beyond scientific computing. (Pronouns: he, him, his).



This site was written in the LISP dialect Scheme (GNU Guile, version 2.2.6) and built with the Haunt library (version 0.2.4) on Thu 04 Jun 2020 16:44:50 BST. The source code can be found on [GitHub]. Under the hood it was heavily inspired by David Thompson's website [dthompson.us].
